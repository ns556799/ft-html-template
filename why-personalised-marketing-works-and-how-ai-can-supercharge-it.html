How to optimise personalisation and win over Gen Z

Gen Z are uniquely placed to influence the future. For brands wishing to engage them, focused segmenting and automation can inspire personalised experiences and loyalty

Technology brings people together – yet its use has displaced some powerful aspects of direct human interaction. This is what makes personalisation so vital. Harnessing the power of the personal touch allows organisations to speak directly to the individuality of their customers – their preferences, lifestyles, tastes and unique experiences – and to do so genuinely.

Gen Z personas

Having come of age at a time of multiple societal, political, and environmental challenges, Gen Z consumers – those born between approximately 1997 and 2012 – tend to prioritise social change, inclusion and sustainability in their decisions. More so than previous generations, they expect the ethics and values of brands to align with their own, and in return organisations are adapting and humanising.

Gen Z don’t want to see performative activism; they want evidence of companies living by their values and prioritising ethical practices. When a company’s values are genuine, they truly resonate as marketing content. To achieve that, effective campaigns need to build on trends, rather than simply following them. That means being relevant for the right reasons, such as educating younger audiences or showcasing a genuine commitment to sustainability.

These ideas are shared by Erifili Gounari, founder and CEO of Gen Z-led social media agency The Z Link. “Brands that aren’t afraid to take risks and experiment with their personalised customer experiences have an advantage,” she says. “Gen Z consumers respond very well to personalisation, so brands that can implement that in their customer journey should always strive to do it.”

“Gen Z consumers respond very well to personalisation”
Erifili Gounari, Founder and CEO, The Z Link

Erifili believes that people assume email is less relevant for Gen Z than it really is. “Recent research found that 53 per cent of Gen Zers enjoy getting emails from their favourite brands once a week,” she says. “So email marketing, when done right and when it’s personalised and tailored, can still work very well.”

Making people feel seen and valued is why personalisation is so crucial for the customer journey. If personalised communication succeeds in conveying meaningful transparency around brand purpose, the reward can be profound: sustained brand loyalty driven by emotional resonance.

Time campaigns with data-led segmentation

Segmenting contact lists and automating personalised emails with focused messaging about organisations’ values, ethics and sustainability-driven practices can be an effective way to empower and engage with Gen Z. Segments are generally thought of as groups of customers with shared characteristics: a specific target audience based on businesses’ data and knowledge of their customers. But there’s a bit more to it than that.

Segments aren’t just the who – they’re also the why, where, and how. Understanding the values, beliefs, lifestyles, and spending habits of Gen Z customers can be key to winning them over. By using advanced personalisation techniques and real-time behavioural data, organisations can utilise Intuit Mailchimp to achieve real success with their Gen Z segments.

Segmentation can also allow brands to extend the life cycle of demand and increase retention. Here, precision is paramount. Predictive modelling offers the capacity to define ultra-specific conditions to identify whether audiences are unengaged customers, midday cart abandoners or loyal customers who can be targeted with personalised offers. Automated flows can save time and drive revenue, and fine-tuned email automations are also a valuable way of connecting with the right audiences at the right time.

“Email, unlike social, is a very personal channel,” says Dee Dunne, Intuit Mailchimp’s Senior Director of Global Lifecycle Marketing and Operations. “You are invited into a person’s inbox, and it’s important to show up in a way that meets their needs.”

“Email, unlike social, is a very personal channel, you are invited into a person’s inbox and it’s important to show up in a way that meets their needs”
Dee Dunne, Senior Director of Global Lifecycle Marketing and Operations, Intuit Mailchimp

Dunne recognises that optimising for the individual is essential. “Gen Z keep a more flexible schedule. Knowing if someone is a night owl or morning lark, and optimising send time to suit their schedule, will increase engagement and impact,” she adds.

Gen Z are spending more time online, across more platforms, than any preceding generation, so an omnichannel approach is essential. If marketers acknowledge the role of each channel in the purchase funnel – integrating email, SMS and social channels – different strategies can be tested out to find the best tool for the job. Sometimes a text may be the most efficient way to follow up an abandoned cart. Maintaining a positive flow across the whole process shows the efficacy and strategy of automating emails for younger audiences. This can provide a basis for generating performance metrics for the Gen Z customer journey experience.

Gen Z put the onus on organisations to drive change, innovation, and new value. In a world of self-service and anonymity, personalisation can positively modulate its customer experience and change the way it interacts with brands.
